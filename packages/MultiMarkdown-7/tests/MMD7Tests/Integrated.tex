\section{Paragraphs}
\label{paragraphs}

Normal paragraphs consist of plain text.

They are separated by empty lines.

Long lines of text will wrap naturally as needed to ensure that they fit in the available space. This will vary by format, of course.

By including two spaces at the end of a line\\
a linebreak is inserted.

MultiMarkdown adds the ability to use\\
an ``escaped newline'' for this as well.

\section{Unordered Lists}
\label{unorderedlists}

Lists can be ``tight'' or ``loose''.

Unordered/tight:

\begin{itemize}
\item foo
\item bar
\item bat
\end{itemize}

Unordered/loose:

\begin{itemize}
\item foo
\item bar
\item bat
\end{itemize}

\section{Ordered Lists}
\label{orderedlists}

Lists can be ``tight'' or ``loose''.

Ordered/tight:

\begin{enumerate}
\item foo
\item bar
\item bat
\end{enumerate}

Ordered/loose:

\begin{enumerate}
\item foo
\item bar
\item bat
\end{enumerate}

\section{Complex Lists}
\label{complexlists}

Lists can contain other elements

\begin{enumerate}

\item sublist
\end{enumerate}
\end{itemize}

\section{Links}
\label{links}

Links can be \href{http://example.com/inline}{inline}\footnote{\href{http://example.com/inline}{http://example.com/inline}}, or \href{http://example.com/reference}{reference}\footnote{\href{http://example.com/reference}{http://example.com/reference}}.

and email \href{mailto:auto@example.com}{auto@example.com}.

\section{Metadata}
\label{metadata}

Metadata can be included at the top of a document.

\begin{verbatim}
Title:		Integrated Test  
Subtitle:	MultiMarkdown test suite  
Author:		Fletcher T. Penney  
\end{verbatim}

It can be referenced with \texttt{[\%title]} to insert the meta value - Integrated Test.
